The Vision

A life lived in slow luxury

Katini's is a deeply personal vision — a retirement sanctuary drawn from the philosophy of Balinese living, where architecture breathes with the landscape, and every corner invites you to pause, exhale, and simply be.

"To age gracefully is to surround yourself with beauty, warmth, and the gentle rhythms of nature."

Inspired by the villas of Seminyak and Ubud, this home weaves natural stone, warm timber, white plaster and open sky into a single, seamless composition — a place that feels both elemental and exquisitely refined.

Design Philosophy

Architecture that breathes

Every element of Katini's is guided by four Balinese principles — a design language that places harmony above all else.

∼

Tri Hita Karana

Harmony between humanity, nature, and the spiritual — the soul of all Balinese design.

◎

Open Living

Indoor and outdoor spaces dissolve into each other through floor-to-ceiling glass and open pavilions.

◇

Natural Materials

Raw stone walls, slatted timber ceilings, polished concrete — textures that age with beauty.

✦

Soft Light

Ambient niches, warm LED accents, and golden hour pools of light throughout every room.

The Layout

Three bedrooms. One world.

A single-storey composition arranged around a private pool and tropical garden — every room connected to light, air, and greenery.

Room by Room

Master Bedroom

Open-plan suite with arched windows, pendant lighting and direct pool access. A bath sanctuary with stone soaking tub.

Bedroom One

Garden-facing room with vaulted timber ceiling, ceiling fan and bespoke arched window framing lush tropical views.

Bedroom Two

A serene retreat with floor-to-ceiling glass opening to a private garden court.

Living & Dining

Open pavilion-style living with sunken seating, slatted wood ceiling and a full kitchen island. Opens entirely to the pool deck.

Pool & Deck

A 9-metre private plunge pool flanked by sun loungers, tropical palms, and a stone feature wall.

Bathrooms

Semi-outdoor wet rooms with natural stone, rainfall showers, and freestanding soaking tubs beside living rock walls.

A Personal Note

This is my dream, taking shape

Katini's is not simply a house — it is the culmination of a life well lived, distilled into stone, wood, water and light. A place to rest, to receive loved ones, and to greet each morning with gratitude.

"I want to grow old here — surrounded by beauty, with bare feet on warm tiles and the sound of water never far away."

∞

The Bali Influence

The architecture draws deeply from the Balinese tradition — where buildings are not imposed on the land but grown from it. Natural materials, open pavilions, sacred geometry, and the constant interplay of water and greenery shape every decision.

The outdoor bath, the stone walls, the tropical garden corridor, the slatted wood ceiling — these are not stylistic flourishes. They are an entire way of living.

A Three-Bedroom Sanctuary

Designed to welcome family and close friends — two guest suites and a master suite, each with their own character and garden connection. The living spaces open fully to the pool, making the outdoors an extension of every room.
